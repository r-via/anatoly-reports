### `rustguard-kmod/src/replay.rs`

| Symbol | Lines | Correction | Conf. | Detail |
|--------|-------|------------|-------|--------|
| `ReplayWindow` | L11–L14 | 🔴 ERROR | 88% | Critical bug in shift_window: the bit-level carry loop iterates `for i in (0..BITMAP_LEN).rev()` — i.e. from index BITMAP_LEN-1 down to 0 — but carry must propagate in the opposite direction. When bits are shifted LEFT within each 64-bit word (moving toward higher bit positions = older counters), any bit at word[i] bit 63 that overflows must land at word[i+1] bit 0 (higher index = older). Achieving this requires iterating 0..BITMAP_LEN (low to high), collecting the overflow from word[i] as carry and applying it to word[i+1] in the next iteration. With the reversed loop, carry from word[i] is instead applied to word[i-1] (newer), and the carry produced by the lowest-indexed word is discarded entirely. Concrete counterexample: bitmap[0] = 0x8000_0000_0000_0000 (bit 63 set, counter top-63 seen), bitmap[1] = 0, bit_shift = 1. Expected result: bitmap[0] = 0, bitmap[1] = 1 (bit 63 moves to word[1] bit 0). Actual result with buggy loop: bitmap[0] = 0, bitmap[1] = 0 — the seen-bit is silently dropped. Any counter whose 'seen' bit sits at bit 63 of a word will have that bit lost on the next window advance that has a non-zero bit_shift, allowing a replayed packet to be accepted. |
